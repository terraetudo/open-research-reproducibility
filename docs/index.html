Open Research & Reproducibility

A practical guide for health and population researchers

Practical guidance for open and reproducible research

Welcome. This site supports the Open Research & Reproducibility workshop for Great Ormond Street Institute of Child Health researchers and staff.
It is designed to be practical, proportionate, and to signpost authoritative guidance where more detail is needed.

Who this is for

PhD researchers, early career researchers, postdoctoral researchers, PIs, and professional staff who want research that is transparent, reusable, and fit for scrutiny.

What this course covers
  • Structuring projects to ensure data, code, and decisions are traceable and reusable.
  • Applying FAIR principles proportionately, including when data cannot be shared openly.
  • Documenting research clearly using READMEs, data dictionaries, ELNs, and SOPs.
  • Improving code quality and reproducibility without over-engineering.
  • Publishing research outputs with appropriate identifiers, licences, and availability statements. .

How to use this site

  1. Work through Chapters 1–10 in order (approximately 15–25 minutes per chapter).
  2. Use Resources and Further Reading for templates, checklists, and UCL-specific guidance.
  3. Apply the practices to one live project as you go (your project README is the anchor).
  4. Use Chapter 11 to consolidate learning and identify next steps.
